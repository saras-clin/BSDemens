[ANALYSIS-1] Time-period analysis method — confirm approach before coding
  The methods plan (section 7e, [B2]) pre-specifies landmark analyses at 1 year
  and 5 years but leaves the implementation approach as TBD. Three options:

  Option A — Time-band IRs and HRs (RECOMMENDED based on literature review)
    Split follow-up into bands: [0–1 yr], [1–5 yr], [5+ yr].
    Compute separate crude IR and adjusted Cox HR for each band.
    Everyone contributes to all bands they were at risk for.
    Most common approach in register-based bariatric surgery outcome studies.
    Directly addresses: early surveillance bias (0–1 yr) vs. sustained effect (5+).
    Aligns with sensitivity analysis 7g.2 (exclusion of first-12-month events).

  Option B — Landmark restriction (more formal)
    At 1-year landmark: exclude all persons who had the outcome or were censored
    before 1 year. Restart analysis from that point in the remaining cohort.
    Changes the study population at each landmark — harder to compare across
    landmarks. Less common in BS/dementia literature.

  Option C — Cumulative incidence at fixed time points (1 yr, 5 yr)
    Report the cumulative probability of dementia by 1 year and 5 years
    (Aalen-Johansen estimator, accounting for competing mortality).
    Descriptive; does not produce HRs. Complement to Kaplan-Meier curves.
    Already partially covered by Figure 2 (CIF plots).

  Current plan in 07_analysis_dementia.R: Option A (time-band analysis).
  Action:   Confirm Option A with supervisors before coding.
            If Option B is preferred, note it modifies the study population
            and estimates must be interpreted as conditional on early survival.
  See:      study1_methods_plan.md section 11 [B2].
