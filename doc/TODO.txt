CRITICAL-1: Pre-surgery N06D exclusion not implemented
  STATUS: Identified but not yet fixed.
  LOCATION: R/01_build_cohorts.R, build_bs_cohort()
  ISSUE: Methods plan criterion #3 states that persons with antidementia
    medication dispensed before the index date (ATC N06D) should be excluded.
    This exclusion is currently missing from build_bs_cohort(). N06D includes
    cholinesterase inhibitors (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine) and memantine
    — medications that are essentially diagnostic of pre-existing dementia.
    Failing to exclude these persons means some prevalent dementia cases remain
    in the exposed group at baseline.
  FIX NEEDED: Add N06D lookback (e.g. 5 years before surgery) to the exclusion
    filter in build_bs_cohort(), alongside the existing ICD-based dementia exclusion.
    Source: lmdb (prescription register), ATC code starting with "N06D".

CRITICAL-2: Death within 30 days after surgery not excluded
  STATUS: Identified but not yet fixed.
  LOCATION: R/01_build_cohorts.R, build_bs_cohort()
  ISSUE: Methods plan exclusion criterion #4 specifies that patients who die
    within 30 days of surgery should be excluded. Currently the code only checks
    whether the patient was alive AT the surgery date (doddato >= surgery_date).
    Patients who die in the post-operative period have near-zero opportunity to
    develop dementia and may represent a systematically different risk profile.
  FIX NEEDED: Add filter: is.na(doddato) | doddato > surgery_date + 30
    in build_bs_cohort() after attaching death dates.

CRITICAL-3: NMI dementia predictors must be excluded for Study 1
  STATUS: IMPLEMENTED (exclude_dementia_predictors = TRUE in extract_nmi).
  LOCATION: R/02_extract_outcomes_covariates.R, extract_nmi()
  CONTEXT: The NMI (Kristensen et al., Clin Epidemiol 2022) includes:
    dx_F00_G30 (F00-F03 / G30 dementia diagnoses, weight = 9) and
    rx_N06D (antidementia medications, weight = 11) as predictors.
    In Study 1 where dementia is the OUTCOME, adjusting for these predictors
    in Cox models would introduce circular confounding (near-perfect collinearity
    with the outcome for incident dementia cases). The exclude_dementia_predictors
    parameter was added and main_extraction() now calls extract_nmi with TRUE.
  NOTE: This means the NMI score used in Study 1 is a modified NMI (48 predictors
    instead of 50). This should be noted in the methods section.

CRITICAL-4: Emigration censoring not implemented
  STATUS: Stub added in R/04_data_management_dementia.R (get_emigration_dates()).
  LOCATION: R/04_data_management_dementia.R, section 4.0
  ISSUE: Persons who emigrate from Denmark leave the Danish registers and can
    no longer be followed for dementia. Treating them as at-risk after emigration
    overstates person-time and may bias hazard ratios if emigration rates differ
    between BS and comparator groups.
  FIX NEEDED: Data manager to confirm register name on DST project 708421.
    Options: VNDS register, BEF-derived (disappearance from annual snapshot),
    or CPR Registeret (RVNDS). Once register is confirmed, replace the stub
    in get_emigration_dates() with the real query.
    emigration_date is already wired into pmin() for censor_date — no further
    code changes needed after the stub is replaced.

MINOR-1: Income quintiles use within-cohort distribution, not population reference
  LOCATION: R/03_extract_ses.R, section 3.2
  ISSUE: SEPLINE (Hjorth et al. 2025) recommends population-standardized income
    quintiles stratified by 5-year age group and sex, using a 3-year average
    income (current year + 2 prior years) to reduce year-to-year variability.
    Currently: within-cohort ntile(5) for a single year.
    Impact: cohort may be systematically higher or lower income than the
    general population, causing quintile boundaries to differ.
  FIX (if pursued): Use IND register for population reference income quintiles
    by age-sex stratum. Requires data manager input on IND column names.
    Also requires computing 3-year average before quintiling.
  REFERENCE: Hjorth et al., Clin Epidemiol 2025;17:593-624, Table 9 and
    supplementary methods.

MINOR-2: sep_category composite (03_extract_ses.R) not recommended by SEPLINE
  LOCATION: R/03_extract_ses.R, section 3.4; R/04_data_management_dementia.R
  ISSUE: SEPLINE explicitly does NOT recommend a composite SEP variable.
    Each dimension (education, income, occupation) captures a distinct aspect
    of social position and should be entered as separate covariates in Cox
    models. The composite sep_category variable currently in ses_data.rds
    conflates these dimensions and may mask heterogeneous effects.
  FIX (if pursued): Remove sep_category from 03_extract_ses.R output.
    In 04_data_management_dementia.R and Cox models: use education_cat,
    income_cat, occupation_cat as separate model terms.
    Update Table 1 structure accordingly.

MINOR-3: OSDC diabetes file covers up to 2022 only
  LOCATION: R/02_extract_outcomes_covariates.R; R/04_data_management_dementia.R
  ISSUE: Persons with diabetes onset in 2023-2024 will be classified as
    No_diabetes in the OSDC file. Affects a small fraction of later surgery dates.
  IMPACT: Minimal — diabetes onset 2023-2024 would be very recent relative to
    surgery date, so few would be misclassified for baseline comorbidity assessment.

MINOR-4: NMI score is a modified NMI for Study 1 (excludes dementia predictors)
  LOCATION: R/02_extract_outcomes_covariates.R, extract_nmi()
  NOTE: For Study 1, exclude_dementia_predictors = TRUE removes dx_F00_G30
    and rx_N06D from the NMI computation (see CRITICAL-3 above). The resulting
    nmi_score is based on 48 predictors, not the original 50. This should be
    described explicitly in the statistical methods section. The modified score
    has not been externally validated — its predictive accuracy for mortality/
    hospitalization may differ slightly from the published NMI.
